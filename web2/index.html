Indian Constitution | Academics

The Indian Constitution is the cornerstone of India's democratic system, adopted in 1950. It establishes the structure of government, dividing powers between the central and state authorities, and guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens. The Constitution promotes equality, justice, and liberty, ensuring the rule of law. It also provides the basis for amending and evolving laws to meet the needs of a diverse and dynamic society.
